# Silver Key Realty — Pathway Specifications

> Each pathway is a dedicated user journey. The landing page identifies user type.
> These pages deliver the experience.

---

## Buyer Pathway (`buyer/index.html`)

**User Profile:** First-time or move-up buyer. Values education. Wants to feel prepared, not pressured.

**Journey Model:** Education-first. Progressive disclosure.

### Required Sections (in order)

1. **Pathway Hero** ✅ (exists as stub)
   - Tag: "I'm Buying"
   - Headline: "Your next chapter starts with understanding."
   - CTA: Request Consultation

2. **Education Framework**
   - "What Most Buyers Don't Know" — 3-card grid
   - Topics: Market reality, hidden costs, timeline expectations
   - Tone: informative, not alarmist. Quiet authority.

3. **The Silver Key Difference**
   - Side-by-side: Traditional Brokerage vs. Silver Key approach
   - Left column: transactional model (what they're used to)
   - Right column: relationship model (what we offer)

4. **Search Criteria Builder** (interactive)
   - Not a property search. A lifestyle questionnaire.
   - Questions: What does your morning look like? How do you use outdoor space?
   - Output: "Your lifestyle profile" — qualitative, not square footage

5. **Attorney Matching Preview**
   - "Every transaction needs legal architecture."
   - Explain the attorney-matching service (connects to Law Pocket)
   - CTA: Learn about legal preparation

6. **Consultation CTA**
   - "Ready to begin? We start with a conversation."
   - Calendar embed or form

### Content Notes
- Never mention price ranges or specific listings
- Education comes before any property discussion
- The buyer should feel smarter after visiting, not more anxious

## Seller Pathway (`seller/index.html`)

**User Profile:** Homeowner considering selling. May feel uncertain about timing or value. Wants to feel their property will be respected.

**Journey Model:** Positioning over listing. Presentation over publication.

### Required Sections (in order)

1. **Pathway Hero** ✅ (exists as stub)
   - Tag: "I'm Selling"
   - Headline: "Your property deserves a presentation."

2. **Why Listing Isn't Enough**
   - The problem with MLS-first approach
   - "Your home is not inventory. It's an asset with a story."
   - Visual: timeline showing traditional vs. Silver Key process

3. **The Presentation Framework**
   - 3 pillars of property positioning:
     - Architectural narrative (what makes this space unique)
     - Community context (neighborhood as value multiplier)
     - Buyer curation (matching, not marketing)

4. **Market Intelligence**
   - "We don't guess. We architect."
   - Placeholder for local market data integration
   - Comparative analysis framework (not specific numbers)

5. **Property Preparation**
   - What Silver Key does before a single showing
   - Photography, staging philosophy, narrative development
   - "We don't photograph rooms. We photograph lifestyles."

6. **Consultation CTA**
   - "Let's discuss what your property deserves."
   - Emphasis: no obligation, no pressure

### Content Notes
- Never make the seller feel their home is "just another listing"
- Positioning language: presentation, curation, architecture
- Avoid: "list", "MLS", "days on market" as primary framing

## Investor Pathway (`investor/index.html`)

**User Profile:** Experienced or aspiring real estate investor. Data-driven but appreciates the lifestyle premium thesis. May be interested in lots, multi-family, or partnership opportunities.

**Journey Model:** Data meets design. ROI with a lifestyle lens.

### Required Sections (in order)

1. **Pathway Hero** ✅ (exists as stub)
   - Tag: "I'm Investing"
   - Headline: "Lifestyle-architected assets that compound."

2. **The Thesis**
   - "Why lifestyle-architected properties outperform"
   - The three-dimensional return: financial + experiential + community
   - This is the intellectual foundation. Make it feel like a research paper, not a brochure.

3. **Asset Classes**
   - Grid of investment categories:
     - Residential lots (Silver Key curated developments)
     - Investment properties (lifestyle premium positioning)
     - Partnership opportunities (development-level entry)
   - Each card: brief description, minimum entry context, timeline expectation

4. **Silver Key Model Deep Dive**
   - Expanded version of landing page model section
   - Development approach, community design methodology
   - How the three-dimensional framework applies to real projects
   - "This is where the thesis becomes tangible."

5. **ROI Framework**
   - Not specific numbers — the methodology
   - How Silver Key evaluates opportunities
   - Three-lens analysis: structure quality, experience potential, community trajectory
   - Visual: framework diagram or matrix

6. **Partnership Inquiry**
   - "For qualified investors exploring development-level opportunities."
   - More selective tone than buyer/seller CTAs
   - Application-style framing, not open-door

### Content Notes
- Investor audience expects sophistication. Don't simplify.
- Data placeholders are fine — the framework matters more than live numbers
- Partnership section should feel exclusive, not salesy

## Rentals Pathway (`rentals/index.html`)

**User Profile:** Someone not ready to buy. May be exploring the area, temporary housing, or just not in buying position yet.

**Journey Model:** Tertiary routing node. Keep it brief. Connect to property management.

### Required Sections

1. **Pathway Hero** ✅ (exists as stub)
   - Tag: "I'm Exploring"
   - Headline: "Not every chapter starts with ownership."

2. **Rental Philosophy** (brief)
   - "Even if you're renting, you deserve intentional living."
   - 2-3 sentences. Don't over-invest in this page.

3. **Property Management Partner**
   - Route to external property management system
   - "Rental inquiries are managed through our trusted partners."
   - External link or contact form

4. **Pathway Redirect**
   - "When you're ready to invest in a permanent chapter..."
   - Links back to Buyer and Investor pathways
   - Keep it warm, not dismissive

### Content Notes
- This page should take 60 seconds to read
- Don't make renters feel like second-class users
- The goal is routing, not retention on this page
